Introduction

Water consumption is increasing every year around the world, endangering future generations. Over time, excessive consumption may lead to shortages, unbalance aquatic ecosystems, and significantly increase water treatment costs as municipalities will have to invest in larger plants. That's why we need to scale up to better manage our daily water use. Here are a few tips to regulate your water consumption

Shower and faucet water

Showers and baths make up another third of our water consumption. Staying in the shower for 10 minutes equals 150 liters of water. The solution ? Opt for an economic knob, because the flow of water goes from 15 to 6 liters per minute.

At the sink, it is also possible to make some savings: remember to turn off the tap when brushing your teeth. Gain: between 5 and 8 liters with each brushing!

Water your garden effectively

For watering without turning your garden into a desert area. To reduce evaporation, it is better to water in the evening rather than in the morning, and especially not in full sun.

Do not mow your lawn too much (6 cm maximum), the tall grass keeps moisture better. And if you can, recover and reuse the rainwater.

Your toilets

Flushing represents more than a third of our total water consumption. A flush uses 6 to 12 liters of water for each use, which represents between 3,500 and 3,800 liters of water per year per person!

A simple way to get around this problem is to slide a 1.5 liter water bottle into your toilet's water tank so that it occupies the extra space. This action saves 3000 liters of water per year per person.

The dishes

The water used for the dishwasher is 13% of the annual consumption of a household. This is a significant figure that can be reduced very simply.

The water consumption of a dishwasher varies from 10 to 20 liters per wash. By doing your dishes by hand, you can divide by 10 to 20 times your consumption. Indeed, it is possible to realize a complete dishes with 1 to 2 liters paying attention to your flow of water.
